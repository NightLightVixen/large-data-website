Owls are mysterious creatures. They’re everywhere yet rarely reveal themselves. Appearing in various culture stories and myths, the owl is also an important icon. Their aura of mystery captivating people for centuries.

If there’s anything you need to know about this fantastic bird of prey, you’ll find it here. Photos, facts, fun and folklore.

There about 222 to 242 species of owl in the world, depending on whos study you read. Owls are classed as mainly nocturnal (active at night) birds belonging to the order Strigiformes, a group that is most closely related to nightjars (Caprimulgiformes). The order is divided into two families: Tytonidae or Barn Owls and related species, of which there are up to 18 species known, and Strigidae, to which the remaining 194+ Owl species belong.
